# Public Movement Strategy

Zidni Academy is a public movement before it is a product. This file fixes the public language so the Founder, the team, and the agents do not drift.

---

## Naming the Founder in public

**Do not** call Mohiyidine "father of AI" or any equivalent self-coronation. That language is fragile — it invites mockery, it ages badly, and it costs more credibility than it buys.

**Use** language that is humbler and, in practice, stronger:

- **Pioneer of AI education in Nouadhibou**
- **رائد نشر ثقافة الذكاء الاصطناعي في نواذيبو**
- **One of the first to bring practical AI literacy to Nouadhibou**

These phrases describe a verifiable action ("first to teach", "first to spread") rather than a claimed identity ("father", "leader"). Action claims survive scrutiny; identity claims do not.

## How the title is earned

The Founder does not give himself the title. The people give it to him — through:

- **Proof** — visible student work, public testimonials, real outcomes.
- **Consistency** — daily presence, daily teaching, daily contact with Nouadhibou.
- **Service** — free challenges, free lessons, accessible language, no gatekeeping of basics.

When enough Nouadhibois say "he taught me AI", the title is real. Until then, claiming it weakens it.

## Tone

- Humble, confident, practical.
- Local first. Speak about Nouadhibou by name. Speak about Mauritania by name.
- Teaching tone over selling tone. The course will sell because the teaching is real, not the other way around.

## What Zidni Academy is, in public

> A public movement to make AI a skill ordinary people in Nouadhibou — and across Mauritania — can use in their daily life and work.

That is the sentence. Everything else is a variation of that sentence.
