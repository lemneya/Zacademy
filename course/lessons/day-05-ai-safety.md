# Day 5 — AI Safety and Verification

**Goal:** Learn when to trust AI and when to check its work.

---

## AI makes mistakes

This is the most important lesson in this course. AI is powerful, but it is not perfect. It can:
- Give wrong facts
- Make up names, dates, or prices
- Give outdated information
- Be too confident about wrong answers

This is called **hallucination** — when AI sounds certain but is actually wrong.

## The verification rule

**Never trust AI with money, health, or legal decisions without checking.**

For everything else, use this simple rule:
> If the answer matters, verify it.

## How to verify

1. **Ask for sources.**
   "Where did you get this information?"

2. **Cross-check with a second AI.**
   Ask the same question to Gemini or Claude. Do the answers match?

3. **Use your own knowledge.**
   If AI tells you the price of fish in Nouadhibou is 500 ouguiya, and you know it is 800, trust yourself.

4. **Check dates.**
   AI knowledge has a cutoff date. It does not know what happened yesterday in Nouadhibou.

5. **Start small.**
   Test AI advice on small decisions before big ones.

## What NOT to share with AI

- Your bank passwords
- Your national ID number
- Other people's private information
- Confidential business secrets you cannot risk leaking

## Today's exercise

**Ask ChatGPT: "What are the current opening hours of the Port de Peche in Nouadhibou?"**

Then:
1. Notice if it knows or admits it does not know
2. Ask: "Are you sure? How do you know?"
3. Write down what you learned about when to trust AI
