# Day 10 — Facebook Posts

**Goal:** Write Facebook posts that get attention and engagement.

---

## Facebook is your billboard

In Nouadhibou, Facebook is where people discover businesses, check prices, and decide what to buy. A good post can bring customers. A bad post is invisible.

## What makes a good Facebook post

1. **First line matters most.** People scroll fast. Your first sentence must stop them.
2. **Use local language and references.** "Port de Peche" beats "harbor." "Cinquième" beats "downtown."
3. **Show, do not just tell.** A photo of fresh fish beats a text description.
4. **Ask a question.** Questions get comments. Comments get reach.
5. **Tell people what to do next.** "Message us" or "Comment below" or "Share with a friend."

## Post types that work

**The story post:**
> "This morning at 5 AM, I was at the Port de Peche choosing the best fish for your lunch. The sun was rising. The fishermen were laughing. This is why I love what I do. Fresh fish, honest price, delivered to your door. Message me before noon."

**The educational post:**
> "3 signs your phone charger is about to break (and how to avoid damage to your phone):
> 1. It gets hot...
> 2. ...
> 3. ...
> Share this with someone who needs to see it."

**The social proof post:**
> "Thank you to Amina from the Cinquième for trusting us with her Eid dress order. She said: 'The quality is better than what I found in Nouakchott, and the price is fair.' Your trust is our reputation."

## AI prompt for Facebook posts

> "Write a Facebook post for my [business type] in Nouadhibou. I want to [goal: sell product / get engagement / announce something]. My audience is [describe audience]. Write in [Arabic/French], local and friendly. Include a hook in the first line, a story or benefit, and a call to action. Keep it under 150 words."
